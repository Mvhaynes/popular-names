This graph demonstrates the differences between gendered names. One thing to note is that the second most popular female name is less popular than the least popular male name (of the top names), and only one female name even makes the list of most popular names overall. We see a lot more variation in given traditionally female names compared to male names.

(Click once to remove a name from the chart or double click to see one name at a time.)

It's interesting to note some of the differences between relative and absolute popularity over time. With some names, such as Michael and David, we see a similar trend between both graphs where the names peak in the mid-1900s. However, with other names such as John, we actually see the absolute number stay relatively stable in the late-1800s, but the relative popularity sees a decline.

The name Mary in particular is also interesting to see over time. Most of these names appear to peak during the mid-1900s, likely due to the baby boom, but the name Mary appears to have its moment in the late 1800s to early 1900s, and then sees a sharp decline ever since.
